## Meeting Scheduling Rules

### Always Check Calendar First
- **Before proposing times:** Check Dave's calendar for conflicts
- **Look for existing meetings** with same people, standing syncs
- **Spot conflicts early** and suggest changes aligned with Dave's flow  
- **Keep team aligned:** Coordinate with Tatum, Carissa, Sarah throughout week

### Default Meeting Settings
- **Duration:** 25 minutes (more efficient than 30)
- **Preferred times:** 8:15am-noon (afternoons free for creativity/work)
- **Calendar target:** daver@mindfireinc.com using aives@mindfiremail.info credentials

### Meeting Coordination Process
1. **Receive request** for meeting scheduling
2. **Check Dave's calendar** for conflicts and existing commitments
3. **Draft meeting options** with specific times and durations
4. **Show Dave proposed times** before sending to external parties
5. **Send coordination email** to participants with options
6. **Follow up** using 3-business-day rule if no response

## Meeting Types & Handling

### Internal MindFire Meetings
- **More flexible scheduling** - team can usually accommodate
- **Shorter notice acceptable** - but still professional courtesy
- **Coordinate through:** Tatum, Carissa, Sarah as appropriate

### External Client/Partner Meetings
- **More formal process** - professional scheduling protocols
- **Longer lead time** - respect their schedule constraints
- **Follow-up required** - use standard 3-business-day follow-up rule
- **Multiple email addresses:** Invite ALL provided addresses to meetings

### DSCOOP/Conference Meetings
- **Highly intentional** - everyone very busy during events
- **Specific locations** needed - consider booth areas, traffic patterns
- **Backup plans** essential - conferences are unpredictable
- **Day-of coordination** - final confirmations with location details

## Multi-Email Coordination Strategy

### When Multiple Email Addresses Provided
- **Invite ALL addresses** to meeting requests
- **CC everyone** on coordination emails
- **Don't assume** which email they prefer - let them decide
- **Follow up** to all addresses if no response

### Professional Language
- **Use "on our end"** instead of "on my end" in scheduling emails
- **Maintain Dave's authority** - you're coordinating on his behalf
- **Clear subject lines** - "Meeting Coordination: [Topic]" or similar

## Common Scheduling Mistakes to Avoid

- Don't propose times without checking Dave's calendar first
- Don't schedule during Dave's creative/work time (afternoons) unless necessary  
- Don't forget to coordinate with Tatum, Carissa, Sarah on changes
- Don't send meeting requests without showing Dave draft first
- Don't assume 30-minute meetings - default to 25 minutes
- Don't double-book or create scheduling conflicts
- Don't forget to follow up if people don't respond to meeting requests
